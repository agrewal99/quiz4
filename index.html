Cancer Prevention and Screening Quiz

1. What is the primary focus of cancer prevention strategies?

Correct Answer: 2. Cancer prevention strategies focus on reducing risk factors and avoiding exposure to carcinogens.

2. Which is an example of primary prevention for cancer?

Correct Answer: 3. Primary prevention involves actions such as lifestyle changes to reduce the risk of developing cancer.

3. What is chemoprevention in cancer prevention?

Correct Answer: 1. Chemoprevention refers to using drugs to prevent cancer, especially in individuals at high risk.

4. Which type of prevention aims to remove precancerous lesions?

Correct Answer: 4. Tertiary prevention involves removing precancerous lesions to prevent invasive cancer.

5. What does secondary prevention of cancer focus on?

Correct Answer: 3. Secondary prevention focuses on early detection of cancer and preventing its progression.

6. Which cancer prevention strategy is described as stimulating the immune system to prevent cancer?

Correct Answer: 2. Immunoprevention involves stimulating the immune system to prevent cancer formation.

7. Which screening method is recommended for individuals at high risk for breast cancer in Ontario?

Correct Answer: 1. High-risk individuals are recommended to have yearly mammography and MRI for breast cancer screening in Ontario.

8. What age group is recommended for biennial breast cancer screening in Ontario?

Correct Answer: 4. Women aged 50 to 74 are recommended for biennial breast cancer screening.

9. What is the primary screening tool used for colorectal cancer detection in average-risk individuals?

Correct Answer: 2. FIT is the primary screening tool for colorectal cancer in average-risk individuals.

10. Which of the following is a benefit of mammographic screening for breast cancer?

Correct Answer: 1. Mammographic screening allows early detection of breast cancer, which improves treatment outcomes.

11. Which factor is considered a modifiable risk for cancer prevention?

Correct Answer: 3. Smoking is a modifiable risk factor that can be changed to reduce cancer risk.

12. What is the aim of opportunistic screening?

Correct Answer: 1. Opportunistic screening is conducted during routine healthcare visits when a patient visits a healthcare provider.

13. What type of cancer does the HPV vaccine primarily prevent?

Correct Answer: 2. The HPV vaccine helps prevent cervical cancer by protecting against the human papillomavirus.

14. Which screening type involves testing individuals without symptoms of a disease?

Correct Answer: 4. Programmatic screening is a standardized approach used to screen individuals without symptoms of a disease.

15. What is the recommended screening tool for individuals at high risk for colorectal cancer?

Correct Answer: 1. Colonoscopy is recommended for individuals at high risk for colorectal cancer.

16. What is a common goal of cancer screening programs?

Correct Answer: 3. The main goal of cancer screening programs is to detect cancer at an early stage, improving treatment outcomes.

17. What age group is eligible for lung cancer screening in Ontario?

Correct Answer: 2. Individuals aged 55 to 74 are eligible for lung cancer screening if they have smoked for at least 20 years.

18. Which cancer screening method reduces the need for dietary restrictions before testing?

Correct Answer: 1. The Fecal Immunochemical Test (FIT) does not require dietary restrictions, unlike gFOBT.

19. Which of the following cancers can be prevented through vaccination?

Correct Answer: 4. Cervical cancer can be prevented through vaccination, specifically with the HPV vaccine.

20. What does the term "chemoprevention" mean in the context of breast cancer?

Correct Answer: 3. Chemoprevention in breast cancer involves using drugs to reduce the risk of developing cancer, especially in high-risk individuals.

21. What is a key component of primary cancer prevention?

Correct Answer: 4. Primary prevention involves avoiding exposure to carcinogens and modifying lifestyle choices to reduce cancer risk.

22. What age group is advised against systematic mammography screening based on Canadian guidelines for breast cancer prevention?

Correct Answer: 2. The guidelines suggest not systematically screening women aged 40 to 49 with mammography, though individual preferences may vary.

23. What type of cancer prevention is aimed at reversing the carcinogenic process?

Correct Answer: 3. Secondary prevention involves slowing, blocking, or reversing the carcinogenic process before cancer becomes invasive.

24. What is the recommended frequency of mammography for women aged 50 to 74 in Ontario?

Correct Answer: 1. Women aged 50 to 74 are advised to undergo mammography every 2 to 3 years.

25. Which cancer type can be detected through a fecal immunochemical test (FIT)?

Correct Answer: 2. The fecal immunochemical test (FIT) is used to screen for colorectal cancer by detecting blood in stool samples.

26. What is the primary goal of lung cancer screening using low-dose computed tomography (LDCT)?

Correct Answer: 3. LDCT is used to detect lung cancer early in high-risk individuals, particularly those with a significant smoking history.

27. Which of the following is a benefit of early cancer detection through screening?

Correct Answer: 4. Early detection of cancer through screening increases the chances of successful treatment and better outcomes.

28. Which statement best describes tertiary cancer prevention?

Correct Answer: 1. Tertiary prevention involves removing or suppressing precancerous lesions to prevent the progression to invasive cancer.

29. Which organization provides guidelines for breast cancer screening in Canada?

Correct Answer: 2. The Canadian Task Force on Preventative Health Care provides guidelines for breast cancer screening in Canada.

30. What lifestyle change is considered a primary prevention measure for lung cancer?

Correct Answer: 3. Quitting smoking is a primary prevention measure for lung cancer, reducing exposure to carcinogens from tobacco.
